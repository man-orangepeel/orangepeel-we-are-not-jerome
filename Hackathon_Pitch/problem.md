# The Problem

## The numbers

- **71%** of American parents give their kids a regular allowance — average **$37/week** *(Wells Fargo, 2025)*
- **53%** of French parents do the same — average **€30/month** *(Fédération Bancaire Française, 2025)*
- UK, Germany: same picture — majority of children receive money on a fixed schedule, unconditionally

That's hundreds of euros per year, per child, with no effort required on their part.

---

## The contradiction

Bitcoin parents spend hours explaining why monetary printing destroys the value of work.  
Then on the 1st of the month, they do the exact same thing with their kids.

Money that falls from the sky, every month, like a birthright.

This isn't a moral failure — it's a structural one. **No tool existed to do otherwise.**

---

## Existing tools fail in two ways

**They replicate the same model.**  
Apps like Greenlight or Pixpay are just digital banks for kids. Bank custody, subscriptions, no real financial education. The underlying mechanic is unchanged: money arrives without effort.

**The Bitcoin education space hits the cold start wall.**  
Every "Bitcoin for kids" app faces the same unsolved question: *who funds the rewards?* A shared pool runs dry. A subscription creates friction. Token rewards carry no real value. The model doesn't scale.

---

## Our answer

Parents already pay. The budget exists.  
We don't create a new money flow. **We fix the one that's already there.**

> *"Orange pill an adult: years of fiat conditioning to undo. An exhausting battle.*  
> *Orange pill an 8-year-old: a foundation for 70 years of sound money thinking."*

The people most worth reaching don't read Bitcoin Twitter. They live in your house.  
The generation that will be 20 when Bitcoin matures is between 5 and 15 years old **today**.
